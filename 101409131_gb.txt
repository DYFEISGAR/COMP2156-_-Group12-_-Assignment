George Brown College is a public, fully accredited college of applied arts and technology located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Founded in 1967, it was named after George Brown, a prominent Canadian politician and one of the Fathers of Confederation. The college offers a wide range of diploma, certificate, and degree programs across various fields such as business, arts and design, community services, early childhood education, construction and engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and culinary arts, and more. George Brown College is known for its strong emphasis on experiential learning, providing students with practical experience through co-op programs, internships, and applied research projects. It has three main campuses situated in downtown Toronto, which are the St. James Campus, Casa Loma Campus, and Waterfront Campus, each offering specialized programs and facilities tailored to the specific needs of its student population.
